Micahel Jordan

About

Micahel Jordan is a professional basketball player that many consider to be one of the greatest if not the greatest player in the history of the game. Micahel Led the Chicago Bulls to a six NBA championships (1991 - 1993, 1996 - 1998) and earned the NBA's Most Valuable Player (MVP) 5 times.

Retirement

After winning his 3rd consecutive championship in 93 Michael Jordan announced his retirement from basketball. In his 93 press conference he stated he had nothing left to prove.

"I'm very solid with my decision of not playing the game of basketball in the NBA," Jordan said. "The reason being — I've heard a lot of different speculation about my reasons for not playing — but I've always stressed to people that have known me and the media that has followed me that when I lose the sense of motivation and the sense to prove something as a basketball player, it's time for me to move away from the game of basketball."

The Last Dance

Michael Jordan's last season with the Chicago Bull was the 1997-98 season. It was also the last time that Michael Jordan, Scotty Pippen, and Dennis Rodman played as a unit. Jordan and the bulls would go on to defeat the Utah Jazz in the NBA finals 4-2 securing a third straight title and 6th overall for Jordan and the Bulls.
